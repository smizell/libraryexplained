Calloway County Public Library Expansion

Timeline FAQ Meetings About

The library board approved a necessary expansion in 2016, but three newly-appointed board members have been working to prevent it from happening.

The current Calloway County Public Library (CCPL) building is underserving the residents of Calloway County in ways that an expansion would alleviate. Public support for the library has not wavered, though. Program attendance has seen a 20% increase since 2015.

The following timeline was created to show when the expansion planning started, and what progress has been made in order to give the residents of Calloway County the library they deserve. It will be updated to reflect any new developments.

Timeline

1969-1976

Calloway County Builds a Public Library

In July of 1969 land was purchased at 710 Main Street for the site of the new library. The work began and the library was completed in 1970. Renovations of the library started in 1975 to add office space, a 150 seat meeting room, a foyer, and a larger parking lot.

The work was completed in 1976 and the library has not been renovated or expanded since .
2005-2011

CCPL Begins Planning for Potential Expansion

The board acknowledges the need for an expansion and creates a building fund. In 2011 the library purchased adjacent property on Olive Street from the county for $1 to be used in an expansion. By 2018 the initial building fund of $14,000 will grow to over 3 million dollars.

2011-2014

Strategic Planning & Evaluating the Community's Needs

CCPL completed its first strategic plan in 2011. Out of that process came five initiatives or areas the library should work on:

  • Educate the public about the library
  • Drive an organizational culture that excels in customer service
  • Define and implement a long-term user-focused facility plan
  • Improve and expand programs, materials, and services
  • Establish framework for self-assessment and identification of best practices.

In 2013 the library updated the plan to include newly developed core values, a vision statement, an updated mission statement, and five key strategies. During a 2014 a Facility Planning Meeting the library formed three commitees to assess Internal Needs, External Best Practices and Financial Feasibility. These three committees were comprised of board members, staff and community stakeholders.

2015

Four Architects

The three committees presented their findings to the board, and it was agreed that the library needed to move forward in planning an expansion. In December 2015 four architects were invited to present an assessment and vision for the current library facility.

  • CMS Architects - Paducah, KY
  • Riley Architect - Mayfield, KY
  • Urban Fabric Design - Murray, KY
  • 5253 Design Group - Louisville, KY
2016

A Design is Chosen

In October 2016, the public was invited to review and discuss two options drafted by Louisville-based 5253 Design Group. Ultimately, the board decided on a compromise of the two options called the "Essential Plus" plan: a 28,000 sq. ft. facility coming in at a completed project cost of $6.4M.

Some have argued that this expansion would increase the taxes collected on behalf of the library; however, in September 2016 the board voted to reduce the tax rate from .064 cents to .059 cents per $100 of assessed property value. Calloway County has one of the lowest special taxing district tax rates in the state of Kentucky.
